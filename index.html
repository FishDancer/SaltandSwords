Abjuration Discipline

Abjuration spells are basically all protection spells. A high proficiency bonus is not required, making Abjuration an excellent secondary or tertiary discipline.

Cantrips

Shield: 1 action, +1 circumstance bonus to AC, +1 circumstance bonus to toughness till your next turn. You also can use the Shield Block reaction until your next turn, which gives you +2 toughness vs 1 attack.


Resistance: 1 action. 30’ range, single target. Target gets a +1 status bonus to the next saving throw it makes durning the next minute.

1 focus point spells

Poison Resistance: 2 actions. Touch range, single target. Target creature becomes resistant 2 to poison damage and has advantage on saving throws against poisons for the next 10 minutes.


Warding Bond: 1 action. Choose 1 creature within 120’ to be the aggressor and 1 creature within 120’ to be the defender. The aggressor makes a Will save. Critical Success the spell has no effect. Success the spell makes the defender resistant 1 to the aggressor’s attacks for 1 minute. Failure the spell makes the defender resistant 2 to the aggressor’s attacks for 1 minute. Critical Failure the spell makes the defender resistant 3 to the aggressor’s attacks for 1 minute. The attacker is aware that this spell made it harder for them to hurt the defender.

2 focus point spells

Magnetic Repulsion: 1 action. You gain a +1 status bonus to AC vs weapons with metal cutting edges for the next 10 minutes. Such weapons include swords, axes, spears, arrows... But it doesn't include clubs, rocks, darts, whips…


Wall of Wind: 2 actions. Conjure a wall of whistling wind that lasts for 1 hour or until you dispel it as an action. Choose 6 consecutive 5’ wide by 5’ long by 10’ tall panels all wholly within 60’ or 1 10’-5’ radius half torus (so a big 10’ radius hemisphere with a small 5’ radius hemisphere cut out of the center) also wholly within 60’. The chosen volume is filled with gale-force wind. And projectiles that pass through the volume have disadvantage to hit. Any creature passing through the volume moves at ¼ speed.

3 focus point spells

Energy Resistance: 2 actions. Touch range, single target. Target creature becomes resistant 3 to Acid, Cold, Electric, Fire, and Sonic damage for 10 minutes.

4 focus point spells

Missile Mirror: 2 actions. Touch range, single target. For the next minute the target is surrounded by a shimmering magical barrier that deflects ranged attacks. All ranged attacks made against this shielded creature have disadvantage. Also ranged attacks that critically miss are rebounded onto the original attacker. These re-bound attacks use your protection spell attack modifier for the attack roll and deal the same damage as the original attack.

5 focus point spells

Wall of Force: 2 actions. Conjure a wall of impenetrable force that lasts for 1 hour or until you dispel it as an action. Choose 6 consecutive 5’ wide by 5’ long by 10’ tall panels all wholly within 60’ or 1 10’-5’ radius half torus (so a big 10’ radius hemisphere with a small 5’ radius hemisphere cut out of the center) also wholly within 60’. A creature that would be encircled or trapped by this gets a reflex save to move to the other side of the wall and not be trapped. The chosen volume is filled with an impenetrable, invisible magical barrier. Nothing except light can go through the barrier. This includes air, so a hemisphere could suffocate the creatures inside if there are a lot of creatures inside.

6 focus point spells

Death Ward: 3 actions. Touch range, single target. The next time during the next hour the target creature is reduced to 0 wounds they go to 1 wound instead.

7 focus point spells

Chromatic Armor: 2 actions. You conjure a suit of magic armor for yourself from pure elemental power. Choose one: Acid, Cold, Electric, Fire, Sonic. You gain resistance 4 to the chosen damage. Also whenever a creature touches you or hits you with a melee attack they take 2D8 of the chosen damage type (they don’t get a save). This spell lasts for 10 minutes.

8 focus point spells

Invulnerability: 2 actions. You are invulnerable to all damage for 1D4 rounds.

Acid Discipline

Acid is primarily a damage discipline. Acid spells tend to be short range and AOE. There are a few utility spells too.

Cantrips

Acid Splash: 2 actions, 60' range, 5' burst, Reflex save vs 2D6 acid damage. If they crit fail the save, also suffering from Acid Burn.


Corrode: 3 actions. Touch range. You slowly apply acid to an object, eventually burning a hole in it with repeated casts. It takes 5 casts to melt through ⅛ of an inch (~3 mm) of iron. Some metals like gold and silver are immune to acid.

1 focus point spells

Acid Arrow: 2 actions. 60' range, single target, attack roll for 2D6+MOD acid damage. Also the target makes a Reflex save. If they fail they become suffering from Acid Burn.


Acid Spray: 2 actions. 15' cone. Reflex save vs 2D8 acid damage. If they fail they become suffering from Acid Burn

2 focus point spells

Acidic Burst: 2 actions. 60' range, 10' burst. Reflex save vs 6D4 Acid damage.


Acid Rain: 3 actions. 150' range, 30' radius, 100' high cylinder. Each creature that starts its turn in the cylinder makes a basic Fortitude save vs 2D8 Acid damage. Any creature that fails is also suffering from Acid Burn. This spell lasts for 10 minutes, or until you end it as a 1 action activity. Any objects or buildings that spend the full 10 minutes will be in varying stages of dissolving.

3 focus point spells

Blinding Foam: 2 actions. 10' range, single target. Spray a sticky acidic foam into the target's eyes. The target makes a Reflex save:

Critical success: they are unaffected.

Success: they are suffering from Acid Burn.

Failure: They are suffering from Acid Burn and are Blind while suffering from said Acid Burn.

Critical Failure: They are suffering from Acid Burn and are Blind for 1 hour.

4 focus point spells

Create Acid: 3 actions. You conjure a small but easily controllable amount of acid that you can easily put into a container. This acid functions as 1 dose of Acid (look at the items section).

5 focus point spells

Acid Wall: 3 actions. You conjure a wall of acid. Choose 6 consecutive 5' by 5' squares. Those squares are filled with a thick wall of acid that reaches 10' into the air. The acid blocks line of sight, but projectiles can still pass through. Any creatures in the area of effect when the acid appears makes a reflex save or is suffering from Acid Burn. Any creature who starts its turn in the acid or passes through the acid must make a basic reflex save vs 8D4 Acid damage.

6 focus point spells

Acid Sphere: 2 actions. Conjure a sphere of acid that acts as your minion. The sphere has 3 wounds, AC 9+spellcasting prof, speed 20, toughness 7, and +spellcasting prof on all saves. It takes half damage from all sources except Sonic and Force and is immune to acid damage and Acid Burn. It has an attack with +spellcasting attack mod to hit that does 2D8 Acid damage with the Acid Burn trait. The sphere disappears if it is reduced to 0 wounds or after 1 minute passes.

7 focus point spells

Corrosive Body: 3 actions. Self. You become covered in potent acid. You are immune to Acid Burn and are Resistant 6 to Acid damage. Also whenever a creature hits you with an unarmed Strike or attempts to Grab, Shove, Trip, or Disarm you or otherwise touch you they become suffering from Acid Burn. Whenever a creature hits you with a melee Strike they take 3D4 Acid damage. Your fists and other unarmed attacks gain the Acid Burn trait. This spell lasts for 10 minutes, or until you dismiss it as a 1 action activity.

8 focus point spells

Disintegrate: 2 actions. 60' range, single target. Basic Fortitude save vs 20D4 Acid damage.

Cold Discipline

Cold is a hybrid discipline. It has ok damaging options and a few defensive and debuff options.

Cantrips

Ray of Frost: 1 action, 120' range, single target, attack roll for 1D6+MOD cold damage. On a critical hit they suffer a -5' penalty to speed for 1 round.


Chill Touch: 2 actions, melee range, single target, attack roll for 3D4+MOD cold damage.

1 focus point spells

Torrent: 1 action. 30' line. Reflex save vs 2D8 Bludgeoning damage. Any Large or smaller creature that fails the save is also pushed back 5', or 10' if they critically fail.


Flash Freeze: 1 reaction. When you take damage, become Resistant 3 to Slashing, Piercing, and Cold damage and Resistant 6 to Fire damage until your next turn as your skin freezes solid. You also become immune to the Burn condition. You are Slowed 1 on your next turn as your skin must melt before you can move.

2 focus point spells

Ice Knife: 2 actions. 60' range, single target, attack roll. The attack deals 1D10+MOD piercing damage. Then, hit or miss, the ice knife explodes on the target. Each creature in a 5' burst centered on the target makes a reflex save vs 2D8 cold damage. These are 2 separate instances of damage.


Ice Armor: 2 actions. Self. For the next 10 minutes your clothes and armor become covered in an extra layer of ice. This grants you Resistance 1 to Slashing, Piercing, and Cold damage, and Resistance 2 to Fire damage. Also when a creature hits you with a melee attack you can use your reaction to shatter the ice, peppering them in sharp splinters. They make a basic reflex save vs 2D4 Cold plus 2D4 piercing damage. After their attack is resolved this spell ends.

3 focus point spells

Snowballs: X actions. Conjure and throw X Snowballs. Each Snowball is 45' range, single target, attack roll. Each does 3D4+MOD cold damage. Each Snowball is a separate instance of damage.

4 focus point spells

Blizzard: 3 actions. 150' range, 30' radius, 100' high cylinder. The cylinder is filled with blinding snow and howling winds. Creatures in the cylinder are Obscured, and all creatures are Obscured to them. Ranged attacks fired through the blizzard have -33% range. All non-magical flames are immediately extinguished when they enter the blizzard and you must attempt to Counteract all magical flames that enter the blizzard (this includes flames from spells like Firebolt or Fireball). The blizzard is Difficult Terrain. Creatures that start their turn inside the blizzard must make a basic Fortitude save vs 4D8 Cold damage. If they fail the save they also suffer a -10' penalty to their speed for 1 round, or 1 minute on a critical failure. The blizzard lasts for 1 minute or until you Dispel it as a 1 action activity.

5 focus point spells

Slow: 2 actions. 60' range, Fortitude saving throw.

Critical Success: not affected.

Success: Slowed 1 for 1 round.

Failure: Slowed 1 for 1 minute.

Critical Failure: Slowed 2 for 1 minute.

6 focus point spells

Cone of Cold: 2 actions. 60' Cone. Basic Fortitude saving throw vs 6D8 Cold damage.

7 focus point spells

Chilling Darkness: 2 actions. 150' range, 20' radius sphere. The sphere is filled with a magical darkness. Attempt to Counteract all magical light inside the sphere or that enters the sphere. When the sphere appears, and whenever a creature ends its turn in the sphere, it makes a basic Fortitude save vs 4D12 Cold damage. Whenever a creature starts its turn in the sphere it must make a Will save. On a success it becomes Frightened 1. On a failure it becomes Frightened 1 and loses 1 action. On a critical failure it becomes Frightened 2 and loses 2 actions. The sphere lasts for 24 hours or until you dismiss it as a 1 action activity.

8 focus point spells

Frost Avatar: 2 actions. Self. You take on a mighty Frost Avatar battle form. This transformation lasts for 10 minutes or until you drop to 0 wounds or dismiss it as a 1 action activity. Your size increases by 1 category, your Toughness increases by 4, your AC decreases by 2, your speed increases by 5', and you gain 2 temporary wounds. You also become Immune to Cold damage, but Weak 4 to Fire damage. You gain the following attacks (which use your spell attack modifier, but don't benefit from any weapon runes or magical foci), but lose all other special actions and weapon attacks (you can still take universal actions):

Frost Spear: 2D6+MOD Cold, Reach, Brace, Trip, Lance (2D6).

Icicle: 45' range, 2D4+MOD Cold, Precision, can create and throw in 1 action.

Freezing Breath: 2 actions, 15' Cone, basic Fort save vs 3D10 Cold damage.

Death Discipline

Basically the mirror image of the life domain. Death spells hurt the living and heal undead

Cantrips

Draining Kiss: 2 actions. Touch range, single target. Deal 4D4 negative damage with a basic fortitude save. If the target fails the save they are also Drained 1 for 1 minute, or Drained 2 for 1 minute on a critical failure.


Touch of the Reaper: 1 action. Touch range, single target. If the target is Dying they make a fortitude save, increasing their dying condition by 1 on a success, 2 on a failure, and 3 on a critical failure. Alternatively, if the target is a Dying undead they recover from the Dying condition.

1 focus point spells

Harm: 2 actions. Touch range, single target, deal 3D8 negative damage with a basic fortitude save or heal 1 wound to undead.


Death Rot: 1 action. Touch range, single target. Make an attack roll. On a hit the target is infected with Death Rot at stage 1 (or stage 2 on a critical hit). You can spend 2 additional focus points any number of times when casting this spell to increase the DC of the death rot by 3 each time.


Death Rot: DC 15, onset 1 day. Stage 1: Carrier, unaffected. Stage 2: Drained 1. Stage 3: Drained 1 and -1 MAX wounds, which only goes away if recover fully. If MAX wounds are reduced to 0, die and rise as a zombie 1 hour later.

2 focus point spells

Extract Life: 2 actions. 30' range, single target. Deal 3D8 negative damage with a basic fortitude save. You regain wounds equal to the number of wounds the target lost.


Vampiric Mist: 3 actions. Conjure a cloud of vampiric mist in a 30' radius sphere centered on a point within 90'. The mist lasts for 10 minutes and is opaque, so it makes creatures Concealed. Any creature that starts its turn in the mist must make a fortitude save. If they succeed they become drained 1 for 1 minute. If they fail they become drained 2 for 1 minute. If they critically fail they become drained 3 for 1 minute. Any creature that ends its turn in the mist makes a basic fortitude save vs 3D4 negative damage.

3 focus point spells

Vampiric Touch: 1 action. Touch range, single target. Target makes a fortitude save. Success: they lose 1 wound. Failure: they lose 1 wound and you regain 1 wound. Critical Failure: they lose 2 wounds and you regain 2 wounds.

4 focus point spells

Greater Harm: 2 actions. Touch range, single target, deal 6D8 negative damage with a basic fortitude save or heal 3 wounds to an undead.

5 focus point spells

Enervation: 2 actions. 30' line. Fortitude save vs 4D12 negative damage. Creatures who fail the save are also Doomed 1 for 1 day, or Doomed 2 for 1 day on a critical failure.

6 focus point spells

Mass Harm: 3 actions. Each creature within 10' is targeted by Greater Harm (so makes a fortitude save vs 6D8 negative damage or heals 3 wounds if undead)

7 focus point spells

Touch of Death: 3 actions. Touch range, single target. The target loses half their current wounds (rounding up), or if they are undead they recover all lost wounds, regrow all lost limbs, and recover from all injuries.

8 focus point spells

Aura of Death: 3 actions. For the next 10 minutes you are surrounded in an aura of harmful magic. On each of your turns pick a creature within 15'. That creature makes a fortitude save vs 3D8 negative damage, or heals 1 wound if it is undead. This is a free action.

Divination Discipline

Divination spells provide useful knowledge. Having a high Divination proficiency is not necessary, making it a great secondary or tertiary discipline.

Cantrips

Detect Magic: 3 actions. Detect any and all magical sources within 60'. You learn which objects and creatures are magic. This spell is blocked by any amount of lead, 1'' of metal, 6'' of stone, and 1' of wood or other organic material.


Ghost Sight: 3 actions. For the next minute, you can see through up to 1” of metal, 6” of stone, and 1' of organic material. Your vision is still completely blocked by any amount of lead.

1 focus point spells

Anticipate Peril: 2 actions. Touch range, single target. Target has a +1 status bonus to all its saving throws for the next 10 minutes.


Approximate: 3 actions. Designate an area wholly within your line of sight and choose a type of thing you would like to approximate. You learn the approximate number of that kind of thing in the area. Examples include cats in a street, people in a building, and goblins under a cloak.

2 focus point spells

See Invisibility: 2 actions. Self. For the next 10 minutes you can see invisible creatures and items as if they were not invisible.


Ill Omen: 2 actions. 30' range, single target. Will save.

Critical Success: unaffected.

Success: disadvantage on their next attack roll.

Failure: disadvantage on their next 2 attack rolls.

Critical Failure: disadvantage on their next 3 attack rolls.

3 focus point spells

Clairvoyance: 3 actions. 500' range. You create an invisible floating eye at a location within range (even if it's outside your line of sight or line of effect). The eye can't move, but you can see in all directions from that point as if using your normal visual senses. The eye lasts for 10 minutes.

4 focus point spells

Behold the Weave: 2 actions. 60' range, single target. You behold the many timelines that weave around a creature, and in so doing, you make one more likely to occur. The target must attempt a Will save.

Critical Success: no effect.

Success: The target must commit all of its actions that it will get next turn. It must take those actions at some point during its next turn: for example “Stride thrice toward Valeros,” “Cast Firebolt,” “Strike at Amiri and Valeros with my jaws.” Those actions are now locked in, and the creature must attempt them, even if the situation has changed—if the creature stated that it would Strike at Amiri with its jaws, it must try to do so. If the creature is entirely unable to fulfill the action it committed to—for instance, if it had only 1 action left and Amiri was out of the range of its jaws Strike—it loses those actions as it reels in a temporal paradox. The order of the actions is not fixed, so they can stride and then strike or strike and then stride if they choose stride and strike.

Failure: as success but the target must commit all actions for the next 2 turns. Those actions must all occur at some point during its next 2 turns, but the order is not fixed.

Critical Failure: As success but the target must commit 3 turns of actions which must occur in its next 3 turns.

5 focus point spells

Mind Reading: 3 actions. Touch range, single target. Will save.

Critical Success: the target learns your surface thoughts.

Success: you learn the target's surface thoughts.

Failure: you learn the target's surface thoughts, and can probe deeper on 1 specific topic. The target makes another Will save. If they fail you learn their thoughts on this topic too.

Critical Failure: you learn the target's surface thoughts, and can probe deeper on 1 specific topic. You learn their thoughts on this topic too.

6 focus point spells

True Target: 1 action. 60' range, up to 6 creatures. You delve into the possible futures of the next few seconds to understand all the ways your foe might avoid harm, then cast out a vision of that future to those around you. Designate a creature also within range (not one of the target creatures). Each target has Advantage on attack rolls made against that creature and automatically passes any flat check required to target the creature due to the creature being Concealed or Hidden. This effect lasts until the start of your next turn.

7 focus point spells

True Seeing: 2 actions. Self. For the next 10 minutes you have True Sight. Creatures with True Sight can see invisible creatures, can see through illusions, can see the auras radiated by magical items and creatures, and can see perfectly in darkness (even magical darkness).

8 focus point spells

Miracle: 3 actions. You manipulate timelines to make a certain miraculous event happen. Make an Arcana check. The miracle can be anything, but the less likely it is the higher the DC becomes. Some example DCs: Finding a gold piece while walking down the street is DC 10. All 6 prison guards leaving their posts to have a shit at the same time is DC 20. Suddenly inheriting a fortune from your second cousin's twice removed aunt because her and like 50 of her extended relatives all died in the same tragic accident, making you her closest living relative is DC 30. Finding out that you already own that one unique and impossible to find magic artifact that you needed to find, and had actually been using it as a fashion piece for years without ever thinking it was even magical at all is DC 40.

Critical Success: the miracle happens as intended.

Success: the miracle happens, but there might be unintended consequences.

Failure: the miracle might happen, but wrong in some way. For instance it might happen to the wrong people, at the wrong time, or in a totally unforeseen way.

Critical Failure: you messed with the timelines without any idea what you were doing. You have altered your timeline irreversibly and almost certainly for the worse.

Electromancy Discipline

Electric spells basically all do damage, making electric an excellent damaging discipline but not useful for anything else

Cantrips

Electric Arc: 2 actions, 30' range, 2 creatures, Reflex save vs 3D4 electric damage. If they crit fail the save, also Flat Footed till the start of your next turn.


Shocking Grasp: 1 action, Touch range, 1 creature. Attack roll for 1D6+MOD electric damage. You have advantage on the attack roll if the target is wearing heavy armor made mostly of metal or is made mostly of metal.

1 focus point spells

Jolt: 1 action, 60' range, single target. Reflex save. Critical Success: unaffected. Success: Shaken +1. Failure: Shaken +1 and Flat-Footed until the start of your next turn. Critical Failure: Shaken +2 and Flat-Footed until the start of your next turn.


Charge: 0 actions. The next time this turn you cast a spell that deals electric damage, increase the number of electric damage die of that spell by 1, and get a +1 status bonus to any attack rolls for the spell and/or the spell's DC. This is considered a status bonus to the number of damage die, so you can't use charge more than once on the same spell or combine it with any other status bonuses.

2 focus point spells

Sudden Bolt: 2 actions. 120' range, single target, reflex save vs 2D10+MOD electric damage.


Lightning Ball: 2 actions. Conjure a ball of electricity that acts as your minion. The ball has 2 wounds, AC 16, speed 20, toughness 6, and +6 on all saves. It is immune to all damage and effects except for force damage. It has an attack with +7 to hit that does 1D6+2 electric damage. The ball can also explode for 2 actions. When it does so, each creature in a 10' radius sphere centered on the ball is targeted by the ball's attack once and then the ball loses 1 wound. The ball disappears if it is reduced to 0 wounds or after 1 minute passes.

3 focus point spells

Lightning Bolt: 2 actions. 120' line. Basic reflex save vs 4D8 electric damage.

4 focus point spells

Chain Lightning: 3 actions. 120' range, 3 ordered targets within 15' of the previous target. The first target makes a basic reflex save vs 4D12 electric damage. Provided the first target did not critically succeed on their save, the second target makes a basic reflex save vs 3D12 electric damage. Provided the first and second targets did not critically succeed on their saves, the third target makes a basic reflex save vs 2D12 electric damage.

5 focus point spells

Pulse Charge: 2 actions. 20' radius sphere centered on caster. Caster is immune, all other creatures make basic reflex saves vs 8D6 electric damage.

6 focus point spells

Storm Avatar: 3 actions. You take on the power of the storm, transforming yourself into a mighty storm avatar. Illusion spells are very useful out of combat and are really only limited by your imagination. They are far less useful in combat

Cantrips

Minor Illusion: 2 actions. Make a stationary silent illusion that fits inside a 10' cube anywhere within 30'. The illusion lasts for 1D6 hours (the DM rolls this secretly). A creature can attempt a perception check vs your Spell DC to see through the illusion.

The disguise can make them look like any other creature, but can't change their apparent weight or height by more than 10%. The disguise lasts for 1D6 hours (the DM should roll this secretly and will let you know when your disguise starts to fade). Creatures can attempt a perception check vs your Spell DC to see through the disguise.


Invisibility: 3 actions. You become invisible (which mechanically means all other creatures are Blind against you) for 10 minutes or until you make an attack or cast a spell.

2 focus point spells

Group Invisibility: 3 actions. You and any creature within 5' of you when you cast this spell become invisible. This functions exactly the same as the regular invisibility spell: a creature becomes invisible (which mechanically means all other creatures are blind against you) for 10 minutes or until they make an attack or cast a spell.


Worm's Repast: 2 actions, 30' range, single target. The target makes a Will save as illusory worms appear and begin consuming its flesh, complete with grotesque munching noises. Critical Success: the spell has no effect.

Fail: the target becomes frightened 2 and stunned 2.

Critical Fail: the target becomes frightened 3 and stunned 3.

3 focus point spells

Each time the illusion is hit the chance of it being hit again goes down by 10%. A creature can use 1 action to make a Perception check vs your spell DC to recognise the real version. They can also point out the illusion to any number of creatures within 30' of them as an action, halving the chance they target the illusion and granting them advantage on any perception checks to identify the illusion. The illusion disappears after 1 minute.

4 focus point spells

You can choose not to include any parts of the cube in the illusion and just leave them looking like they normally do. A creature can see through the illusion by succeeding on a Perception check vs your spell DC. They have Advantage on the check if they are within 30' of the illusion.

6 focus point spells

It can speak and uses your spellcasting attack modifier for its Deception, Diplomacy, Intimidation, and Performance checks. A creature can see through the illusion by succeeding on a Perception check vs your spell DC. They have disadvantage on the check unless they have some reason to be suspicious of the Doppleganger.

7 focus point spells

If any hallucination is particularly unbelievable the target gets to make another Will save, ending the effect on a critical success.

They become Clumsy 1 and Enfeebled 1 for 24 hours while they adjust to their new body, which is now permanently disfigured. Critical failure: They become Clumsy 1 and Enfeebled 1 and are permanently disfigured.

Beast Form: 2 actions. Take on a battle form of a fierce beast. This transformation lasts 1 hour, until you end it as a 1 action activity, or you go unconscious. You gain 1 temporary wound and must choose a form to use. You gain the listed things, but lose all your other abilities. Note “prof” refers to your Transfiguration Spell Proficiency:

Wolf: Toughness 7, AC 14+prof, speed 35'. Medium creature

Bite: +4+prof, 1D10+4 piercing.

Ape: Toughness 8, AC 13+prof, speed 30', Climb 25'. Medium creature

Fist: +4+prof, 1D6+4 bludgeoning, Forceful, Grab, Disarm, Trip.

Crocodile: Toughness 9, AC 12+prof, speed 25', swim 25'. Large creature

Bite: +4+prof, 1D12+4 piercing.

Great Eagle: Toughness 6, AC 13+prof, speed 25', 40' fly. Can carry 10 bulk while flying. Small creature

Peck: +3+prof, 1D8+3 piercing.

4 focus point spells

Stoneskin: 2 actions. Touch range. Turn a target creature's skin into hard stone, greatly increasing their resistance to weapons. The creature becomes resistant 5 to Piercing and Slashing damage for 10 minutes.

6 focus point spells

Polymorph: 3 actions. Polymorph a creature into another creature of the same or lower tier. They lose all their abilities and stats, but gain all the abilities and stats of their new form. This transformation lasts for 10 minutes. If their new form is reduced to 0 wounds they revert to their normal form but lose half (rounding up) their maximum wounds (so they end up with however many wounds they had before polymorphing -½ their maximum wounds). Unwilling creatures get a Will save to resist the effects. Polymorph also attempts to Counteract any other spells or effects altering a creature's form. If the counteract fails then the creature returns to its altered form when polymorph ends.

7 focus point spells

Flesh to Stone: 2 actions. Melee range, single target, fortitude save. Critical success: the target is unaffected. Success: the target becomes Slowed 1 for 1 minute as their flesh briefly hardens. Failure: the target becomes Slowed 2 for 1 minute as their flesh hardens. Critical Failure: the creature's flesh turns to stone and they become petrified. They are not dead, merely suspended in stone until this spell is dispelled. They cannot move, speak, see, or otherwise interact or do anything while petrified. If their stone body is damaged that damage will remain if this spell is dispelled.

8 focus point spells

True Polymorph: 3 actions. Polymorph a creature into another creature of the same or lower tier. They lose all their abilities and stats, but gain all the abilities and stats of their new form. Unwilling creatures get a Will save to resist the effects. A polymorphed creature reduced to 0 wounds doesn't revert to their normal form; instead they follow normal Dying rules for their new form. This transformation lasts until dispelled by you as an action or is counteracted. True Polymorph also attempts to Counteract any other spells or effects altering a creature's form. If the counteract fails then the creature returns to its altered form if true polymorph ends.

Rituals

Rituals have some DC. When casting a ritual you need to make the listed checks and beat the listed DCs (arcana most used skill for arcane rituals). If any of the checks are failed the ritual fails and the components are wasted. Critically failing these checks can also have other worse effects. Some rituals require multiple casters, known as assistants, who make additional checks which and must pass their checks to succeed at being assistants.


If a ritual ever causes a creature to make a saving throw, the DC of the save is the highest among the caster's DCs for all skills used to cast the ritual.

Trained

Tier 1 rituals are almost automatic and have very few, if any, material components


Mage Hand: 10 minute ritual. 1 S cost. 1 DC 16 Arcana check. Summon a floating hand made of pure force. It is visible as a spectral floating hand. The hand can lift 5 bulk and has a thievery modifier equal to your arcana modifier-2, and has a -4 on all other skills. It acts as your dependent minion, has 1 wound, toughness 1, AC 8+your arcana modifier, and is immune to Poison and Mental damage. It has a 15' speed and fails all saving throws. The hand disappears when reduced to 0 wounds or when you create another hand.


Mage Armor: 10 minute ritual. No cost. No checks. For the next 24 hours your unarmored type armor has 2 less MAX stat bonus to AC, but has 1 additional innate toughness and AC.


Mending: 10 minute ritual. X cost. 1 DC 14 Arcana or crafting check (caster's choice). Repair 1 broken item of cost 10*X.


Unseen Servant: 10 minute ritual. 1 G cost. No checks. Create a small invisible servant that acts as your minion. It has hands and can pick things up, but has a -4 modifier on all skills so will not be good at doing anything technical. The servant can only carry 10 bulk, has 1 wound, 1 toughness, AC 8+your arcana modifier, and is immune to Poison and Mental damage. It has a 15' speed and fails all saving throws. The servant disappears when reduced to 0 wounds or you create another servant.


Alarm: 10 minute ritual. No cost. No checks. Designate a door or up to 6 consecutive 5'*5' squares. You may also designate a passphrase. When a creature opens or closes the door or enters one of the squares without uttering the passphrase you receive a mental alarm. You are unable to distinguish between different alarms you set. So if you set multiple alarms and 1 gets triggered you will not know which.


Lock: 10 minute ritual. 1 G cost. DC 14 Arcana check. Pick a non-magical lock. Increase the number of checks required to open said lock by 1 and the DC of the lock by 2.


Perpetual Flame: 10 minute ritual. 1 G cost. DC 14 Arcana check. Create a small magical flame that emits no heat or smoke. The flame lasts perpetually and consumes no oxygen. If you critically succeed on the check you can create a fancy colored flame.


Message: 10 minute ritual. No cost. DC 15 Arcana check. Utter a message of 25 words or less and choose a creature you know and have met in person. They hear your message unless they are on a different plane of existence.


Divine Light: 1 hour ritual. No cost. DC 15 Arcana check. Designate a 15' radius, 100' high cylinder. For the next 24 hours that area is filled with magical light which counts as sunlight.


Dispel Magic: 10 minute ritual. No cost. DC 12 Arcana check. Counteract a spell or ongoing magical effect. You have disadvantage on your counteract check if you are trying to counteract an ongoing magical effect instead of a spell.


Summon Familiar: 10 minute ritual. 5 S. DC 15 Arcana check. Summon a spirit in the form of a small animal. It does your bidding and acts as a Dependent minion. It has 1 wound, terrible AC, and can't make meaningful attacks. You can communicate with each other telepathically as long as you are within 120' of each other. The familiar disappears when reduced to 0 wounds or when you create another hand.


Druidcraft: 10 minute ritual. No cost. DC 12 Nature check. Make a small plant sprout from a nearby surface or invigorate a plant, making it grow twice as fast as normal and produce twice as much produce as normal (a plant can only be invigorated by magic once).


Plant Shaping: 4 hour ritual. 1 G. DC 15 Nature check. Choose a plant. You alter the shape of the plant to something of your choosing, but can't increase the weight of the plant. It is common on some islands to make houses inside the biggest trees in this way.


Water Breathing: 30 minute ritual. 4 S. DC 15 Arcana check. Choose up to 6 creatures who remain close to you while you are performing the ritual. That creature can breathe underwater for the next hour.

Expert

Slightly harder rituals that normally have some costly material components.


Translate: 1 hour ritual. Enough ink and paper to write down the translated message. 1 DC 16 Arcana check and 1 DC 16 Society check. Perfectly translate up to 2,000 words of text in any language and transcribe it into any other language. You don't have to speak either language (the original language and the language to translate to), but for each of the 2 languages you speak, reduce the DC of both checks by 2.


Speak with Dead: 1 hour ritual. No cost. 1 DC 18 spellcasting check. Designate 1 corpse with a functioning mouth. At the end of the ritual the mouth and head animate for 10 minutes. During that time it can speak, see, and hear, but remembers only what the creature knew in life. It is under no obligation to speak the truth or answer any questions you ask.


Bestial Curse: 1 hour ritual. A piece of an animal. 1 DC 16 spellcasting check. You imbue the animal component of the ritual with a curse that partially transforms whoever touches it into that animal. Here “animal” refers to any creature made of living flesh that has no innate magical abilities. Cat, Shark, Human, Owlbear count. Tree, Golem, Demon, Dragon, do not. Anyone who touches the cursed animal body part must make a fortitude save. Critical Success: they are unaffected by the curse. They can touch the body part as often as they like to no ill effect. Success: They are partially affected. Their body changes slightly as it tries to transform. The changes are relatively minor, but they do become clumsy 1 for 2 weeks as they adjust to the subtle changes. The changes are permanent. Failure: They are affected. The results are as success, but the changes are slightly more drastic and they are Clumsy 2 for 2 weeks and Clumsy 1 for an additional 2 weeks after. Critical failure: They succumb to the curse, transforming into a rough copy of the animal whose body part it is. Their base physical stats (Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Toughness, and Speed) change in relation to the base physical stats for the animal, and they gain any natural abilities of the animal (e.g. a flying speed for a bird, a jaws attack for a wolf…). They also lose any abilities inherent to their previous form (race abilities, opposable thumbs, ability to speak words…). They retain their intelligence, personality, memories, tier, and mental statistics. They also are Clumsy 2 for 2 weeks and Clumsy 1 for an additional 2 weeks after.


Glyph of Warding: 1 hour ritual. 4 G cost. 1 DC 15 spellcasting check. Designate a door or up to 6 consecutive 5'*5' squares. Also choose Acid, Cold, Electric, or Fire. You may also designate a passphrase. When a creature opens or closes the door or enters one of the squares without uttering the passphrase the glyph explodes, dealing 6D4 of the chosen damage in a 10' burst, 15' cone, or 30' line with a basic reflex save.


Darkvision: 1 hour. No cost. 1 DC 15 spellcasting check. 1 creature gains the Darkvision ability for the next 8 hours.


Magic Circle: 1 hour ritual. 6 G cost. 1 DC 18 spellcasting check. Designate a 10' or less radius circle that you trace on the floor. Also choose a creature type. Creatures of the chosen type cannot cross the line of the magic circle; they are prevented by an impenetrable invisible barrier that doesn't do anything to other creatures.


Revivify: 1 hour ritual. 5 G cost. 1 DC 20 Arcana or Religious Lore check. 1 creature who died in the last 24 hours and whose soul is free and willing is brought back to life on 1 wound. This does not heal any bodily ailments, so they will probably die again without healing.


Sending: 10 minute ritual. 1 G cost. 1 DC 18 Arcana check. Can have 1 assistant, who speaks the message to be sent. Send a message (less than a minute of you or your assistant speaking) to a creature. You have to have met the creature receiving the message or know their true name (just their actual full name, not any nickname or false name). The receiving creature must be on the same plane of existence.


Plant Growth: 4 hour ritual. 8 G cost. 1 DC 16 Arcana check and 1 DC 16 Nature check. You invigorate tha plants, making them grow stronger and faster. For the next year all plants within 1001' grow twice as fast, twice as big, and produce twice as much produce.


Consecrate: 1 hour ritual. 12 G cost. 1 DC 20 Arcana check or 1 DC 20 Religious Lore check. You consecrate an area to a certain being or deity, allowing them greater influence over the area. The area can be no more than a 101' radius sphere, but you can choose any smaller area. Most deities choose to invigorate their worshipers while they are on consecrated grounds and forbid their sworn enemies from entering their consecrated grounds.


Desecrate: 1 hour ritual. 12 G cost. 1 DC 20 Arcana check or 1 DC 20 Religious Lore check. You desecrate an area, purging all influence a deity/being had over the area (such as a Consecrated area). The deity/being no longer has any special attachment to the area and will be unable to influence it in the way they used to. This does nothing if the area has no deific influence.


Augury: 1 hour ritual. 6 G cost. 1 DC 18 Arcana/religious lore check. Contact a powerful being such as a deity and ask them a yes or no question. They will respond with yes, no, or unclear. It's advised to choose a nice being who won't take pleasure in blatantly lying or purposefully misleading you.


Water Walk: 1 hour ritual. X G cost. 1 DC 20 Arcana check. Up to X target creatures can walk on water as if it were extremely thick and sticky mud for the next 4 hours. It counts as Difficult terrain.


Master

These are pretty difficult rituals, often requiring 2 checks and a respectable investment in material components.


Commune with Nature: 4 hour ritual. No cost. 1 DC 22 Nature check and 1 DC 22 Arcana check. For the next 24 hours you can communicate with all natural living things within 1 mile of the casting site. So you can talk to plants and animals, they will understand you, and you will understand their responses.


Magic Mailbox: 4 hour ritual. 16 G cost plus 2 small containers. 2 DC 20 Arcana checks. The 2 containers provided for components are now linked. Any object placed in one is transported instantly to the other, and vice versa.


Magic Weapon: 1 hour ritual. 18 G cost. 2 DC 22 Arcana checks. 1 NON-magic weapon you touch during the ritual becomes magic. It has a +1 item bonus to hit and a +1 item bonus to damage. It loses 2 rune slots as this enchantment is not compatible with runes.


Planar Message: 1 hour ritual. No cost. 2 DC 19 Arcana checks. Choose a creature you know, or speak a creature's true name. Then speak a short message (less than 1 minute long). The chosen creature receives the message telepathically, regardless of the intervening distance or being on different planes. The target can also respond with a short message.


Create Undead: 4 hour ritual. 12 G cost. 2 DC 22 Arcana checks. When you cast this ritual you can choose to pay half the casting cost for -1 point, double the casting cost for +1 point, +5 DC for +1 point, -5 DC for -1 point. Choose a corpse. You also get +1 point for each tier the corpse was (+1 for tier 1 creature, +3 for tier 3) and +1 point for each tier you are. That corpse becomes animated as either a Skeleton or Zombie depending on its level of decay. At base it is tier 0, but you can spend 2 points to make it tier 1, 5 points to make it tier 2, or 8 points to make it tier 3. At base it is a dependent minion under your control, which means it follows your commands to the best of its ability (note undead tend to be rather stupid so it might misunderstand complicated commands). You can spend 1 point to make it an independent minion instead. Note undead have terrible memories so they will forget their commands pretty quickly (it is good practice to give undead new orders every 24 hours). You can spend 1 point to make it have a good memory, meaning it will never forget your commands. The corpse animated by this ritual retains some of its physical stats and abilities from when it was alive, but no magical abilities. So an animated rat will have the physical stats of a rat. An animated humanoid will have the physical stats of that humanoid. An animated griffin will have the physical stats of a griffin, and will likely be able to fly.


Cup of Dust: 6 hour ritual. 20 G cost, and 1 cup, tankard, goblet, or other drinking vessel. 2 DC 21 Arcana or Occult Lore checks. The cup used for the material component becomes terribly cursed. Any amount of liquid ingested from the cup dehydrates the drinker just as much as it would have hydrated from drinking pure water. Note this does not change the liquid in any way; so it will taste the same and have all the same properties. It can also be removed from the cup and drunk from another vessel and will then hydrate as normal. Furthermore because of how dehydration works any amount drunk from the cup will normally make the drinker Fatigued. And if a creature only drinks from the cup their maximum number of wounds is reduced by 1 for each day they continue to drink from the cup. A creature whose maximum wounds is ever 0 instantly dies.


Telepathic Link: 2 hour ritual. No cost. 2 DC 19 Arcana checks. At the end of the ritual touch a creature. For the next 24 hours you and that creature are telepathically linked. This means you can converse with each other silently and across vast distances.


Teleportation Circle: 1 hour ritual. 12 G. 2 DC 22 Arcana checks. Half cost if leaving from an already made permanent teleportation circle. At the end of the ritual you and any creatures or objects inside the circle you draw during this ritual are teleported to a permanent teleportation circle who's identifying code you know. Teleportation circles are normally quite fancy, but you can make a basic one with its own unique identifying code by performing this ritual and not teleporting anything. Making a circle with the same code as an already existing one is a serious problem as now anyone traveling to either circle goes to a random circle. Hence mages are very reluctant to share their teleportation circle codes.


Scrying: 1 hour ritual. 12 G or a 60 G crystal ball that is reusable. 2 Arcana checks vs the Will DC of the chosen creature (or DC 18 if a place is chosen). Choose a creature or place. If you have met the creature, know the creature's true name, or have been to the place you need only make 1 check. This ritual creates an invisible sensor somewhere nearby the creature/location. You can see through the sensor as if it were a window with a 360 degree view. The sensor also picks up and transmits sound as if your ear were in the place of the sensor. The sensor disappears after 10 minutes or you dismiss it as an action. If the creature or place does not exist or is on a different plane, or you critically fail one of the checks you can be shown something random and probably unhelpful/misleading.


Forbiddance: 4 hour ritual. 24 G. 2 DC 20 Arcana checks. Designate an area that fits within a 101' radius sphere. All Teleportation in and out of the area, Scrying within the area, and Divination effects to learn about the area are Counteracted using your Arcana modifier with Advantage.


Hex: 2 hour ritual. 6 G and a piece of the target creature's body. A drop of blood or single hair suffices. 2 Arcana checks vs the target's Will DC. The target creature becomes Hexed. This is a powerful curse that makes them Weak 1 to all damage, Doomed 1, and gives them Disadvantage on all skill checks using a chosen statistic.


Divine Guidance: 2 hour ritual. 12 G. 1 DC 20 Arcana check and 1 DC 20 Religious Lore check. You contact a deity and implore them to guide your path. You can tell the deity what you need guidance on by stating a goal you have that you need guidance to accomplish, but ultimately they will select a creature, object, or place they think you should visit. Normally it will help you towards your stated goal, but sometimes the deity will point you towards something totally unrelated if they think it will help you more or just want to prank you. You will know the rough direction of said creature, object, or place until you do or accomplish whatever the deity thought you should do or accomplish with the creature/object/place.


Mage's Tower: 6 hour ritual. 36 G. 2 DC 20 Arcana checks. You conjure a magical tower. The tower must fit within a 100' tall 30' radius cylinder, but its exact shape is up to you. The walls, floor, and interior of the tower are made of pure magical force, but come with illusions that make them look like whatever you decide. The tower is immune to all damage, but can be dispelled by powerful enough magic users. The tower can have doors and windows but lacks any fine details (like locks, or intricate artwork) and is totally unfurnished (no mattresses, sheets, pillows, cooking utensils, ect.).

Legendary

Tier 4 rituals are incredibly powerful but are very expensive and extremely difficult to perform correctly. Even the most powerful and learned magic users struggle to cast them.


Absorb Magic: 1 hour ritual. No cost. 1 DC 25 Arcana check. Can have any number of assistants, who each make 1 DC 20 spellcasting check. You and all assistants regain all lost focus points. Can use a diamond worth 100G, which halves the time of the ritual (the diamond isn't destroyed, it just helps the process of absorbing the magic). Can also use a permanent magic item, which also halves the time of the ritual but increases the DC of your check to the DC of the item (or 25, whichever is higher), increases the number of checks you have to make by 1, and increased the DC of the assistants' checks to 5 lower than the item's DC (or 20, whichever is higher). This disables the item for X hours, where X is the total number of focus points regained amongst you and the assistants.


Spell Immunity: 1 hour ritual. 32 G cost. 3 DC 25 Arcana checks. Can have 1 assistant, who makes 1 DC 20 spellcasting check. Name a spell. For the next 24 hours you or your assistant are completely immune to that spell. A creature can only be immune in this way to 1 spell at any given time. If you are already under the effect of the spell you are immune to its effects for 24 hours.


Raise Undead: 12 hour ritual. 100 G cost. 3 DC 25 Arcana checks. Can have any number of assistants, who must each make 1 DC 22 spellcasting check. Each corpse within 1 mile becomes animated a tier 0,1, or 2 Skeleton or Zombie depending on its level of decay and tier (tier 0 creatures become tier 0, tier 1 and 2 become tier 1, tier 3 and 4 become tier 2). They are not under anyone's control and will act completely of their own accord (which means they will attack the closest living creatures). However, the undead animated by this ritual view you and any of your assistants as allies and will not attack you nor your assistants.


Creation: 8 hour ritual. No cost. 3 DC 23 Arcana checks. Create 100 bulk of wood, stone, metal, water, or flesh. It appears out of nothing but is totally real.


Planar Binding: 6 hour ritual. 50 G cost. 3 DC 24 spellcasting checks. Choose a creature. That creature must remain within 30' of you throughout the ritual, and cannot change planes during the ritual as long as it is within 30' of you. At the end of the ritual that creature is bound to the current plane. It cannot use any form of planar travel while bound. Note summon spells tend to summon creatures from different planes, and when the summon spell ends the summoned creature returns to its native plane. So if you summon a creature, then bind it to the current lane it cannot return to its native plane and the summon spell can not end.


Curse of Death: 6 hour ritual. 80 G cost. 3 DC 26 Arcana or Occult Lore checks. Can have 1 assistant who must make 1 DC 26 spellcasting check. If you have an assistant you have advantage on your checks. At the end of the ritual speak a creature's ture name. That creature makes a fortitude save vs your Arcana or Occult Lore DC (whichever is higher). If they Critically Succeed: they are unaffected. Succeed they become Doomed 1. Fail: they become Doomed 2. Critically Fail: Doomed 3.


Globe of Invulnerability: 24 hour ritual. 120 G cost. 2 DC 30 Arcana checks. Can have 2 assistants who must each make 1 DC 25 spellcasting check. If you have 2 assistants you have advantage on your checks. Create a perfect sphere of any radius less than 1 mile centered on the square you are standing on when you finish the ritual. Nothing may enter or leave the sphere unless you make an exception for it during the ritual. For instance you could allow yourself and your assistants to enter and exit. You could also allow anyone who speaks a certain passphrase to enter or exit. You could allow air to come and go freely so anyone inside doesn't suffocate.


Resurrection: 6 hour ritual. 100 G cost. 3 DC 25 Arcana or Religious Lore checks. Choose a corpse you can touch throughout the ritual. If that corpse's soul is free and willing it is returned to the corpse and then the corpse is brought back to life. The body is also cured of all mortal wounds and any missing body parts are regrown. Can also be used on alive creatures to regrow missing body parts. This costs half as much and takes half as long.


True Polymorph: 4 hour ritual. 60 G cost. 3 DC 28 Arcana checks. Can have an assistant who doesn't need to make any checks and gives you Advantage on your checks. If you had an assistant that assistant is polymorphed into a creature of their choice. If you did not have an assistant you or a willing creature you touch is polymorphed instead. You can also select an unwilling creature but they get a Will save vs your Arcana DC to resist. Polymorphed means their physical statistics become those of the chosen creature. They lose all their class abilities, but gain any class abilities of the chosen creature. This polymorph lasts until dispelled, which can only be done by something like the Dispel Magic ritual.


Puppet: 12 hour ritual. 80 G cost and a puppet vaguely resembling the target creature that contains some part of the target's body (a drop of blood or single hair suffices). 3 Arcana checks vs the target's Will DC. If you do not know the target's true name your checks are made at Disadvantage. The target creature becomes linked to the puppet. Whenever the puppet moves, so too does the target creature. Whenever the puppet takes damage, so too does the target creature (puppet has AC 10). It takes an action to manipulate the puppet, which forces the target's body to also move in the same way.


Sphere of Annihilation: 24 hour ritual. 100 G cost. 2 DC 30 Arcana checks. Can have 2 assistants who must each make 1 DC 25 spellcasting check. If you have 2 assistants you have advantage on your checks. Create a perfect sphere of any radius less than 1 mile centered on the square you are standing on when you finish the ritual. Every living thing inside the sphere makes a Fortitude save vs your Arcana DC. You and your assistants treat your saves as 1 degree of success higher. Critical Success: take 30 Negative damage. Success: take 60 Negative damage. Failure: take 120 Negative damage. Critical Failure: take 240 Negative damage.


Revelation: 12 hour ritual. 101 G cost. The ultimate divination ritual. You behold the cosmos in all its infinite variations and focus on the most likely version of reality. You can observe any 6 hours of that reality at any point in space of time. Can be non-consecutive hours. Bear in mind that reality might not occur, especially if you take steps to prevent or alter it.


Devil's Sight: 4 hour ritual. 50 G cost and the blood of a sentient creature killed within the last 24 hours. 3 DC 25 Arcana or Occult Lore checks. You gain Devil's Sight, a powerful form of Darkvision that can see through any amount of normal and magical darkness.
